After running all of the DFS, BFS, DLS, IDDFS searches to solve the 4 3 waterjug problem these are the results:

Number of steps:
- DFS and DLS performed very performed the same, having 10 total steps, including the initial state and goal state and reaching a depth of 6.
- IDDFS and  BFS performed the same, having to go basically level by level, these search algorithms took a lot more steps to reach the goal state.

Memory:
- DFS and DLS require the least memory, shown by the number of steps
- BFS and IDDFS require more memory, BFS requiring the most memory

Optimality:
- DFS and DLS are not optimal as it works to just find the goal state not the shortest path
- BFS is optimal as it works to find the shortest path as it goes level by level
- IDDFS also gives an optimal path as it has a limit and then epxlores the next levels given.
